A:They must use "ungood" for "bad" and "uncold" for "warm".

A:Newspeak is expected to supersede Oldspeak by about the year 2050. The final adoption was fixed for a late date chiefly to allow enough time for the preliminary work of translating pre-revolutionary literature and technical manuals into Newspeak.

6.
Q: What was the main purpose of Newspeak?
A: The main purpose of Newspeak was to express the worldview of Ingsoc while making all other modes of thought impossible, especially heretical thoughts against the Party.

7.
Q: Why was the vocabulary of Newspeak deliberately reduced?
A: The vocabulary of Newspeak was reduced in order to diminish the range of thought. By cutting down the number of available words, the Party aimed to make unorthodox ideas harder or impossible to express.

8.
Q: How was the word “free” used in Newspeak?
A: In Newspeak, “free” could only be used in concrete senses, such as “free from lice” or “free from weeds.” It could not mean political or intellectual freedom, because those concepts no longer existed in Newspeak.

9.
Q: What were the three classes of Newspeak vocabulary?
A: The three classes of Newspeak vocabulary were the A vocabulary, the B vocabulary, and the C vocabulary.

10.
Q: What was the A vocabulary used for?
A: The A vocabulary was used for ordinary everyday activities such as eating, drinking, working, dressing, riding in vehicles, gardening, and cooking. It was not suitable for literature, politics, or philosophy.

11.
Q: What was special about Newspeak grammar?
A: Newspeak grammar was highly regular and allowed words to be used interchangeably as nouns, verbs, adjectives, or adverbs. For example, “think” could serve as both noun and verb, replacing the older word “thought.”

12.
Q: How did Newspeak form negative and intensified meanings?
A: Newspeak formed negative meanings by adding “un-,” as in “ungood.” It formed stronger meanings with “plus-” and “doubleplus-,” as in “pluscold” and “doublepluscold.”

13.
Q: What was the B vocabulary designed for?
A: The B vocabulary was designed for political purposes. Its words carried ideological meanings and were intended to impose a desired mental attitude on the person using them.

14.
Q: What does the word “goodthink” mean in Newspeak?
A: “Goodthink” roughly means orthodoxy or thinking in an orthodox manner according to the principles of Ingsoc.

15.
Q: Why were compound words and abbreviations important in Newspeak?
A: Compound words and abbreviations were important because they made speech short, rapid, and easy to pronounce while reducing unwanted associations. Words like “Minitrue” were more controllable than longer phrases like “Ministry of Truth.”

16.
Q: What is “duckspeak” in Newspeak?
A: “Duckspeak” means to speak automatically, like a duck quacking. When used for orthodox opinions, it was considered a compliment, meaning that the speaker could produce correct Party opinions without conscious thought.

17.
Q: What was the C vocabulary used for?
A: The C vocabulary consisted of scientific and technical terms. These words were used by specialists, but there was no general vocabulary for science as a method of thought.

18.
Q: Why did Newspeak have no word for “Science” as a general concept?
A: Newspeak had no word for “Science” as a general concept because science as an independent habit of mind or method of thought was not allowed. Any acceptable meaning of science was considered to be covered by Ingsoc.

19.
Q: Why would the sentence “All mans are equal” lose its political meaning in Newspeak?
A: In Newspeak, “equal” no longer had the meaning of political equality. The sentence could only mean that all men were physically equal in size, weight, or strength, which would be obviously false.

20.
Q: Why could old literature not be fully translated into Newspeak?
A: Old literature could not be fully translated into Newspeak because many of its ideas depended on concepts that Newspeak had removed. Translating such texts would require changing their meaning to fit the ideology of Ingsoc.
